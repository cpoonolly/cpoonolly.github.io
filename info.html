I was born in Kerala, India with the name Cherry. Cherry was a pretty common name in Kerala & I was actually named after my grandfather (his name was Cherian).

Unfortunately when my family moved to the US, my parents quickly realized that they had inadvertantly named their son after a fruit... So, in an attempt to spare me a life of ridicule, they renamed me to Ryan. ('Ryan' comes from the 'rian' in 'Cherian')

Despite their efforts, I would find plenty of other ways to subject myself to ridicule over the years (ex: my hairstyle choice in high school/college definitely didn't help the situation). So ultimately I resolved to just stick with Cherry.

The end result? I'm officially Ryan on paper, but everyone calls me Cherry!

..which is good, I prefer Cherry :)

Honest Buildings is a asset management platform used by some of the largest owners in real estate in the industry. Our clients included companies such Vornado Reality Trust, Brookfield, SL Green Reality, Beacon Capital Partners, etc.

I initially joined HB 3 years ago and had the chance to watch the company grow from ~15 to over 100 people. Being apart of HB during this period of growth was an incredibly valuable experience. I learned a lot about how to scale up a business & a team.

After delivering several high impact projects including our primary user dashboard, our permissions system, cost tracking, invoicing, etc. I was eventually given the opportunity to lead a small squad of 4.

Working closely with clients, our team was able to build some very cool features including a new capital planning tool and the infrastructure for multi-language support (internationalization).

I joined Goldman Sachs after graduating college. I had previously worked there as a summer intern & was given a return offer at the end of the summer.

At GS my team's role was to calculate firmwide exposure to counterparty default (i.e. counterparty risk).

Basically we were answering the question: "if 'CompanyX' goes bankrupt how much money could the firm as a whole lose?".

Different GS trading desks would then be charged a fee based on how much counterparty risk they carry & the money from this fee would be used to hedge their positions.
